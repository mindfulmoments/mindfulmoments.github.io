Pain Relief
relaxation training


A poll in 2005 suggested that about half of American suffer from chonic or recurrent pain, and nearly half (46%) say they have had pain in the last two weeks (Stanford Univeristy Medical Poll Center). Common treatments for pain include medical evaluations and pain reduction medications. However, some people even after surgery to reduce pain and the use of multible pain killers still experience pain daily. Prescription and non-prescription medications are not a long-term solutions to chronic pain because the body can develop tolerance to pain killers.

Behavioral therapy provides an alternative for chronic pain suffers. After the patient has had a medical evaluation, learning techniques to relax and learning different ways to think about pain can make pain more tolerable. Patients report feeling more in control of their lives after having mastered these techniques. Common pains that are effectively treated with behavioral therapy include: back pain, knee pain, headaches, and pain from illnesses such as fibromialgia and IBS. You can learn how to reduce your pain without additional medications. Call Eunice for to find out if your pain could be reduced with behavioral therapy.
