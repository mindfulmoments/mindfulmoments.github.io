Awake Refreshed Tomorrow
learn ways to improve your sleep habits


60% of American report problems sleeping. 20% of Americans report sleeping 6 hours or less per night (National Sleep Foundation, March 2011). Sleep problems reported include trouble falling asleep, waking in the night, not being able to return to sleep after waking, waking too early in the morning, sleep walking, snoring, or feeling un-refreshed when waking up in the morning.

Many Americans turn to medications to help: 25% of Americans take some kind of sleep aide. But medications have side effects, and in some cases require higher doses over time. Behavioral therapy (including relaxation training and changes to sleep habits) is also an effective treatment for insomnia and other sleep problems. Patients who use techniques learned in therapy report feeling more in control of their lives and more refreshed when they wake up. Therapy is generally short-term and often results in life-long changes to sleep patterns. Call Eunice to schedule an assessment.

Insurance usually covers treatment costs if the patient has a diagnosed sleep disorder. Patients are encourage to first have a medical doctor's evalation before beginning behavioral treatment for sleep disorders. Behavioral therapy is usually effective after four sessions. Patients are asked to do at least ten minutes of homework daily during the course of treatment.
