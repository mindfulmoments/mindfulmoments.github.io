Better Sleep
Refreshing sleep without medications


60% of American report problems sleeping. 20% of Americans report sleeping 6 hours or less per night (National Sleep Foundation, March 2011). Sleep problems reported include trouble falling asleep, waking in the night, not being able to return to sleep after waking, waking too early in the morning, sleep walking, snoring,or feeling un-refreshed when waking up in the morning.

If you have trouble sleeping, you're in good company. Many Americans turn to medications to help with sleep problems. 25% of Americans take some kind of sleep aide to help them sleep. But medications have side effects and in some cases require higher doses over time. Behavioral therapy (including relaxation training and changes to sleep habits) is also an effective treatment for insomnia and other sleep problems. Patients who use techniques learned in therapy report feeling more in control of their lives and more refreshed when they wake up. Therapy is generally short-term and often results in life-long changes to sleep patterns.

The seminar will teach you some basic mindfulness and other relaxation techniques. Particpants will also discuss their successes in small groups encouraging learning from one another. A three hour seminar (or three one hour classes) are offered to local groups. Cost vary depending on the size of the group. Topics include: creating a relaxing sleep area, tracking your sleep cycle, learning to "turn off" racing thoughts, effects of anxiety and depression on sleep, mindfulness (see menu item at right), relaxation exercises to use before sleep, helping children to develop good sleep habits, and how sleep needs change for seniors over age 65. A 45 min. seminar is available to non-profit groups in Seneca for no charge on two of the above topics. Contact Eunice to discuss your group's needs and to schedule a seminar.
