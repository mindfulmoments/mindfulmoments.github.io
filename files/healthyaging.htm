Healthy Aging
A seminar for seniors and caregivers of seniors
There are now more Americans age 65 and older than at any other time in U.S. history. Over 40 million people in the US are over age 65, and the population is expected to increase at a faster rate than the total population over the next decade as more baby boomers turn 65. That means seniors make up about 13% of the total population (US Census Bureau report 2010). Seniors remain active long after 65 and continue to seek out ways to learn, grow, and contribute to their families and communities. Modern psychological research has much to teach seniors about living healthy and full lives.

This seminar will help you learn ways to keep your brain active and flexible, handle stress better, sleep better, manage pain without increasing medications, keep connected to extended family, be a better caregiver for the the frail, prepare for death and end of life decisions, notice the signs of dementia, and pass on your faith. Eunice has worked with many families facing health problems or caring for the frail elderly. She'll share tips she learned on staying healthier and being a better caregiver. One hour seminars can also be designed around only one or two of the topics, or a longer seminar or series of classes can be offered. This seminar is ideal for seniors groups or caregivers groups.

Choose from the following topics: the difference between dementia, delerium, and Alzheimers; keeping your brain active and fit; questions to ask your doctor; local resources for seniors; volunteering after 60; staying connected to grandchildren; passing on your faith; living wills and ethical wills; and the body/mind connection.

One hour seminars are free for non-profit groups in the Seneca area. For other groups, seminars costs vary based on the number of particpants.
